Ok, so to be honest 300 is a bit tight for a reflex camera, if you want an acceptable one (not for beginners) you might want to spend a bit more and get a good one. If it is for a friend maybe is better to give her the money and she can add the rest and buy a better one or maybe go for another present , otherwise she might feel it is not the camera she wanted and it is a lot of money to get something just ok or similar of what she already has. If she already has a reflex camera would be nice to know which one to see if we can improve the one that she has with this budget

Having said that, below are the options that we think can work for her for 300 or a bit more (we struggle to get options for 300). Your match is the Canon EOS 1200 basically because is the one closer to your budget but the Nikons are better cameras as you can see on the comparison table that includes their design, being lighter and thinner. My Telecom offers the Canon with III Lens for your budget and Amazon UK for 15€ more ( be aware it could come with the UK plug to charge the battery- adapters are cheap) if you want to buy it in a more renowned Website.
Note: The IS II Lens and the III Lens share the same optics so image is sharp but the III doesn't include image stability which depending of what the camera is going to be used for, could be a disadvantage.

Another option is go for the body alone and let her pick the lens but in general is cheaper to buy them together.
